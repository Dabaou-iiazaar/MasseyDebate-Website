Spring 2019 @ University of Karl Zhu


What is MasseyHacks?


MasseyHacks is an event where high school students come together for a fun-filled weekend of hacking! We welcome students of all skill levels: from printing "Hello World", to seasoned coders. We have experienced mentors and workshop hosts onsite to provide an enriching educational experience for those who are just starting as hackers. MasseyHacks is an event where you can let your imagination run wild, create anything related to technology, and win cool prizes at the same time.

FAQ

What is a hackathon?

It is an event where students, both experienced and beginners in programming, gather together and build cool projects with technology.

Do I need to know how to program?

No, not at all! If you've never programmed before, we'll teach you to write your very first line of code!

What should I bring?

You should bring your laptop (we won't provide computers) & charger, school ID, phone & charger, and power strips (recommended). Food will be provided!

How will the teams work?

You are encouraged to find teams before the event, but don't worry if you don't have one, we will help you find a team at the event! You can also choose to hack alone, but it's always more fun with a team! Teams can have maximum 4 people.

I know a company that wants to sponsor, what should I tell them?

Awesome! Let them know that they can contact us at sponsorship@masseyhacks.ca!

How much does it cost?

Nothing! MasseyHacks is completely free to everyone attending! Oh, yeah, we forgot to mention FREE FOOD AND SWAG!

How do I know if I got accepted?

We will be doing 4 rounds of acceptances every Sunday starting March 25th. To be eligible for that round, you must submit your application before 11:59 the Sunday before. If you do not get accepted in the round, you do not need to re-apply, your application moves to the next round. If accepted, we will email you with details.

How do I get to MasseyHacks V?

If you are a high school student in the Windsor-Essex area, you should find your own transportation. We are sending a bus from the University of Toronto - St. George that will also be stopping at the University of Waterloo. Unfortunately, we will not be providing other travel reimbursements.

Code of Conduct?

Yes, of course! All hackers must abide by the MLH Code of Conduct found here.
